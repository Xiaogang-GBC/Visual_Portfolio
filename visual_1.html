Dream Deferred: A Data Story on Immigration Policy Changes

This project, “Dream Deferred,” explores the effects of shifting immigration policies on international students in Canada. As an international student myself, I focus on illustrating the emotional and economic impact of these changes. Using public datasets and iterative visualizations, my work highlights trends over the past decade, from student enrollment fluctuations to rejected applications and their broader economic consequences. By integrating compelling storytelling with data, the visuals aim to resonate with policymakers, educators, and the public. Through interactive web-based formats and personal narratives, this visualization emphasizes the human cost of these policies while advocating for sustainable, transparent immigration reforms.
